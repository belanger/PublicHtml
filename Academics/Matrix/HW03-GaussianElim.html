Gaussian Elimination
Homework 3

Show all steps and identify each row operation in working the following problems.

  1. Use back-substitution to find all solutions of the following systems.
    1. \[\begin{array}{rcrcrcr} x &+& 3y &+& 5z &=& 3\\ & & y &-& 2z &=& 2\\ & & & & z &=& 1 \end{array}\]


      \(\vectr{x\\y\\z} = \vectr{-14\\4\\1}\)

    2. \[\begin{array}{rcrcrcr} x &-& 2y &+& z &=& 4\\ & & y &+& 3z &=& 1\\ & & & & z &=& 2 \end{array}\]


      \(\vectr{x\\y\\z} = \vectr{-8\\-5\\2}\)

    3. \[\begin{array}{rcrcrcr} x &+& y &+& 3z &=& 4\\ & & & & z &=& 2 \end{array}\]


      \(\vectr{x\\y\\z} = \vectr{-2-y\\y\\2} = \vectr{-2\\0\\2} + y\vectr{-1\\1\\0}\)
      where \(y\) can be anything (it is free).

    4. \[\begin{array}{rcrcrcrcr} x_1 &+& 3x_2 &+& x_3 &-& x_4 &=& 1\\ & & x_2 &-& 4x_3 &+& 2x_4 &=& 2\\ & & & & & & x_4 &=& 5 \end{array}\]


      \(\vectr{x_1\\x_2\\x_3\\x_4} = \vectr{30-13x_{3}\\4x_{3}-8\\x_3\\5}= \vectr{30\\-8\\0\\5} + x_3\vectr{-13\\4\\1\\0}\) where \(x_3\) can be anything (it is free).

  2. Put the following matrices in row-echelon form. (Use Gaussian elimination; do it one step at a time.)
    1. \[\left(\begin{array}{cccc} 2 & 4 & 6 & 2\\ 1 & 3 & 8 & 1\\ 2 & 2 & 3 & 4 \end{array}\right)\]


      \[\left(\begin{array}{cccc} 2 & 4 & 6 & 2\\ 0 & 1 & 5 & 0\\ 0 & 0 & 7 & 2 \end{array}\right)\]

    2. \[\left(\begin{array}{cccc} 0 & 1 & 3 & 4\\ 1 & 3 & 8 & 1\\ 2 & 7 & 19 & 7 \end{array}\right)\]


      One solution is \[\left(\begin{array}{cccc} 1 & 3 & 8 & 1\\ 0 & 1 & 3 & 4\\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 \end{array}\right)\]

  3. Use Gaussian elimination and back-substitution (if necessary) to find all solutions of the following systems.
    1. \[\begin{array}{rcrcrcr} 2x &+& 4y &+& 2z &=& 12\\ x &+& y &-& 3z &=& 0\\ 3x &-& 3y &+& 4z &=& 1 \end{array}\]


      \(\vectr{x\\y\\z} = \vectr{1\\2\\1}\)

    2. \[\begin{array}{rcrcrcr} x &+& 3y &-& 2z &=& 5\\ 2x &-& y &+& 2z &=& 7\\ x &+& 10y &-& 8z &=& 3 \end{array}\]


      No solution.

    3. \[\begin{array}{rcrcrcr} 2x &+& 2y &+& 4z &=& 6\\ x &+& y &+& 3z &=& 2\\ 4x &+& 4y &+& 10z &=& 10 \end{array}\]


      \(\vectr{x\\y\\z} = \vectr{5-y\\y\\-1} = \vectr{5\\0\\-1} + y \vectr{-1\\1\\0}\) where \(y\) can be anything (it is free).

  4. The general equation for a circle in the plane is \[ Ax^2 + Ay^2 + Bx + Cy + D = 0.\]
    1. If a circle passes through the three points \((3,2)\), \((-1,2)\) and \((1,0)\), then \(A\), \(B\) and \(C\) will satisfy \[\begin{array}{rcrcrcrcr} 13A &+& 3B &+& 2C &+& D &=& 0\\ 5A &-& B &+&2C &+& D &=& 0\\ A &+& B & & &+& D &=& 0\\ \end{array}\] Find an equation for the circle. (\(D\) will be a free variable; you can find a specific solution by setting \(D=1\), for example.)


      \(x^2 + y^2 -2x - 4y +1 = 0\)

    2. Find an equation of the circle passing through the points \((4,5)\), \((6,1)\) and \((5,4)\)


    \(x^2 + y^2 -2x - 2y -23 = 0\)

  5. Find all solutions of the following system of equations. \[\begin{array}{rcrcrcrcr} \frac{2}{x} &+& \frac{3}{y} &+& \frac{4}{z} &=& 13\\ \frac{1}{x} &+& \frac{2}{y} &+& \frac{1}{z} &=& 6\\ \frac{3}{x} && &+& \frac{1}{z} &=& 10\\ \end{array}\] (Note: Since this system is not linear, you’ll want to solve for something related to \(x\), \(y\) and \(z\), and then solve for \(x\), \(y\) and \(z\).)


    \(\vectr{x\\y\\z} = \vectr{1/3\\1\\1}\)
