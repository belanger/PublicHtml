Average Rates of Change
Homework 1
  1. Find the average rate of change of y=f(x)=2x2 from x=1 to x=3.
    Δ y/Δ x = 8.
  2. Find the average rate of change of y=f(x)=√x from x=1 to x=9.
    Δ y/Δ x = 1/4.
  3. To buy x pounds of new, improved, Stuff, it costs f(x)=3√x dollars. What is the average rate of change of the price (with respect to the number of pounds bought) from x=1 pound to x=4 pounds?
    The average rate of change is 1 dollar/pound.
  4. An object is traveling so that in t minutes, its position will be f(t)=2t2−4t+3 feet away. What is the average velocity of the object from t=2 to t=5 minutes?
    The average velocity will be 10 feet/minute.
  5. A car is traveling away so that in t hours, it will be f(t)=45 + 2t2 miles away. What is the average velocity of the car from now (t=0) to t=2 hours?
    The average velocity will be 4 miles/hour.
  6. An object is travelling so that in t minutes, it’s position will be f(t) = 2t2−4t+3 feet away. What is the average velocity of the object from t=2 to t=5 minutes?
    The average velocity will be 10 feet/minute.

  7. As x increases from 3 to 7, the average rate of change of y with respect to x is 5. How much does y increase? (In other words, what is Δ y?)
    Δ y = 20.
  8. As x increases from 2 to 5, the average rate of change of y=f(x) with respect to x is 3.
    1. What is Δ y?
      Δ y = 9.
    2. If f(2)=7, what is f(5)?
      f(5)= 16.
  9. From time 2 hours from now until 4 hours from now, a car’s average velocity is 45 miles per hour.
    1. How far does the car travel during this time?
      The car will travel 90 miles.
    2. If the car is 100 miles away in 2 hours, how far away is it in 4 hours?
      The car will be 190 miles (assuming it is traveling away.)
